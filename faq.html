Common Questions About TDS & Tax Calculation (FY 2025-26)

1. What is TDS in salary?

TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) is income tax deducted by your employer from your monthly salary based on your annual income and deductions.

2. Which tax regime is better for FY 2025-26?

Compare both regimes using TheFinvia calculator. Choose the old regime if your deductions are high (80C, HRA, etc.). New regime offers lower slabs with fewer deductions.

3. How do I calculate TDS for my income?

Use TheFinvia TDS Calculator by entering your income, deductions (like 80C, PF, HRA), age group, and tax regime.

4. What is the standard deduction in the new regime?

For FY 2025–26, the standard deduction is ₹75,000 for both old and new regime salaried taxpayers.

5. Is HRA allowed in the new tax regime?

No. HRA, along with most deductions like 80C, 80D, LTA, is not allowed in the new regime.

6. What is the limit of Section 80C deductions?

Maximum deduction under Section 80C is ₹1,50,000 for investments in PPF, LIC, ELSS, home loan principal, etc.

7. Can I switch regimes every year?

Yes. Salaried employees can change the tax regime every financial year while filing their income tax return (ITR).

8. What is rebate under Section 87A?

You get a rebate up to ₹25,000 under the new regime if taxable income is under ₹7 lakh. Under the old regime, rebate is ₹12,500 for income up to ₹5 lakh.

9. What is the due date for TDS return filing?

Quarterly TDS returns are to be filed in Form 24Q. Q4 returns (Jan–Mar) are due by May 31st.

10. Can freelancers or business owners use this tool?

Yes. Anyone with taxable income can use TheFinvia to estimate annual and monthly TDS even without a fixed employer.

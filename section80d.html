Section 80D – Tax Deduction on Health Insurance

Home Section 80C Section 80D FAQ

What is Section 80D?

Section 80D of the Income Tax Act provides deductions for premiums paid on health insurance policies for yourself, your spouse, children, and dependent parents — including senior citizens. You can also claim deductions for preventive health check-ups.

Who is Eligible to Claim?

  • Self, spouse, and dependent children
  • Parents (whether dependent or not)
  • Senior citizens (age 60 and above) with or without insurance

Deduction Limits under Section 80D for FY 2025–26

Category Maximum Deduction (₹)
Self + Family (below 60) ₹25,000
Parents (below 60) ₹25,000
Parents (above 60) ₹50,000
Self + Family (below 60) + Parents (above 60) ₹75,000
Both Self & Parents (above 60) ₹1,00,000

Preventive Health Check-up

You can also claim up to ₹5,000 for preventive health check-ups within the overall Section 80D limit. These check-ups can be for any member of the family or parents.

Important Notes

  • Premiums must be paid via non-cash modes (except preventive check-ups)
  • Medical expenses for senior citizens (without insurance) are eligible up to ₹50,000
  • Always retain receipts and payment proof for ITR filing

Section 80D is one of the most effective ways to reduce tax while ensuring health protection for your family. Include it in your annual tax planning strategy.

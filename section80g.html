What is Section 80G?

Section 80G of the Income Tax Act allows taxpayers in India to claim deductions on donations made to specified charitable institutions, relief funds, or NGOs. These deductions reduce your total taxable income, thus saving tax legally.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Applicable to all taxpayers: individuals, HUFs, firms, companies, etc.
  • Donation must be made via cheque, draft, or digital payment (not cash above ₹2,000).
  • Donation receipt must contain PAN of the trust/institution and your name.

Types of Donations and Deduction Limits

Donation Category Deduction Allowed Examples
100% without limit 100% of donated amount PM CARES Fund, National Defence Fund
50% without limit 50% of donated amount Jawaharlal Nehru Memorial Fund
100% with 10% limit 100% up to 10% of adjusted gross total income Government/Local Authority for promoting family planning
50% with 10% limit 50% up to 10% of adjusted gross total income NGOs and charitable trusts

How to Claim 80G in FY 2025-26

  • Collect donation receipt with PAN & 80G registration number.
  • Mention donation details in your ITR under “Deductions under Chapter VI-A”.
  • Ensure the institution is listed under approved 80G entities by the Income Tax Department.

Important Notes

  • Donations in kind (clothes, food, etc.) are not eligible for deduction.
  • Donations to foreign NGOs are not covered.
  • Check for updated list of approved institutions before donating.

Section 80G not only helps you save tax but also promotes social welfare by encouraging charitable giving.
